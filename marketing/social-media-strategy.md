# Social Media Strategy

A strong social media presence is crucial for building trust, providing social proof, and answering customer queries. Coin Care Capital will maintain an active presence across four primary platforms.

## 1. TikTok (Primary Top-of-Funnel)
TikTok is the most effective platform in Kenya for viral reach and brand awareness. We will use a "Creator-led" approach.

*   **Content Pillars:**
    *   **"Day in the Life":** Behind-the-scenes looks at loan officers valuing cars and disbursing funds in under 24 hours.
    *   **Financial Education:** Quick tips on how to manage business cash flow, understand logbook loans vs. bank loans, and avoid predatory lenders.
    *   **Customer Success Stories:** (With permission) short interviews with business owners who used our loans to clear stock or expand.
    *   **Humor/Trends:** Participating in localized Kenyan corporate TikTok trends to humanize the brand.
*   **Format:** Vertical video, 15-60 seconds, fast-paced editing, local trending audio.
*   **Posting Frequency:** 3-4 times per week.

## 2. Instagram (Visual Trust & B2B)
Instagram serves as a digital storefront. Prospective clients will check Instagram to ensure the company is legitimate, active, and has an office.

*   **Content Pillars:**
    *   **High-Quality Graphics:** Informational carousels (e.g., "5 Documents You Need for a Logbook Loan").
    *   **Reels:** Cross-posted from TikTok but optimized with professional covers.
    *   **Stories:** Daily updates, "Office vibes", Q&A stickers, and sharing customer reviews.
    *   **Highlights:** "How to Apply", "FAQs", "Our Office", "Testimonials".
*   **Format:** Square graphics, Carousels, Reels, and Stories.
*   **Posting Frequency:** 3 times per week on feed, daily on Stories.

## 3. Facebook (Community & Middle-of-Funnel)
Facebook remains a massive driver of traffic in Kenya, especially for an older demographic (35-55) who are more likely to own vehicles.

*   **Content Pillars:**
    *   **Long-form Education:** Detailed posts linking back to the website's blog.
    *   **Community Management:** Rapid responses to comments and direct messages via Facebook Messenger (often the primary way older users will ask "how much can I get for a Mazda Demio 2015?").
    *   **Trust Signals:** Photos of the office, staff members, and official Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) regulation badges.
*   **Format:** Image + Link posts, text updates, localized Kenyan Swahili/Sheng engagement where appropriate.
*   **Posting Frequency:** 3-4 times per week.

## 4. X / Twitter (Customer Service & Authority)
X is used primarily for rapid customer service, addressing PR concerns, and establishing industry authority among corporate Kenyans.

*   **Content Pillars:**
    *   **Customer Support:** Dedicated response hours for queries and complaints.
    *   **Industry Commentary:** Threads on the state of SME financing in Kenya, car ownership statistics, and economic tips.
    *   **Company News:** Announcements regarding new branch openings, changes in interest rates, or new loan products.
*   **Format:** Text threads, quote tweets, and infographics.
*   **Posting Frequency:** 1-2 times daily (mix of proactive tweets and reactive replies).

## Core Community Management Guidelines
*   **Response Time:** Aim for < 1 hour during business hours on all DMs and comments.
*   **Tone of Voice:** Professional, empathetic, transparent, and distinctly Kenyan. Avoid overly formal banking jargon; speak like a trusted financial advisor.
*   **Handling Negativity:** Never delete negative comments unless they are abusive or spam. Address complaints publicly by asking the user to DM their phone number to resolve the issue offline.
